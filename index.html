Leprosy

of the land

Thousands hectares of woodland turned into lunar landscapes due to illegal amber mining. We created machine model to search for traces of such activity on satellite images for the area of 70 thousands square kilometers.

Ukraine has a world amber market share of 30-40%, it is from 250 to 350 tons.

Ukrainian amber "klondikes" provide jobs to thousands of people who live near gem deposits in Volyn, Zhytomyr, and most of all in Rivne region

A few decades ago one could hardly imagine how to make profit out of the solar stone. People would dig up amber in their vegetable gardens and burn it in stoves as a fuel for house heating.

Everything changed in 2012-13. The town hotels of Rivnenska oblast have never seen so many Chinese people before. Foreigners brought currency and smuggled the amber back to China. The Chinese buyers paid generously, so the amber mining got mechanized — shovels gave way to power pumps. It was the golden age for amber diggers leading to environmental disaster. All pictures in this article are the satellite images of amber mining sites.

According to approximate calculations, about 50 000 of Ukrainians are engaged in illegal amber mining.

Like top-performing miners of old Soviet times, the amber area residents head on to "work shifts". An estimated 30,000 of Ukrainians of different age and professions are engaged in amber digging: senior grade school pupils, retired people, there are even teachers and clergymen. They operate on more than 5,500 hectares of land. Annual revenues from illegal mining, according to the government officials, amounts to USD 120-300 million.

90 per cent of the Polissya amber is exported to China, the rest is distributed between the United Arab Emirates, Turkey and European countries. China is the key price setter for the Ukrainian amber.

Annual revenue from illegal mining, according to government estimates, is USD 120-300 million.

However, prior to reaching the end customer, the Ukrainian amber is legalized in Poland and sold at the Gdansk stock market. It means that the Polish intermediaries receive a lion's share of profits, investing them in the processing, scientific research, legal trade. Illegality of mining takes a major part of potential profits from domestic amber diggers, but they do not seem to be concerned about it.

In 2016 a new supplier for China and the new competitor for Ukraine emerged. Kaliningrad amber factory has struck a deal with the Chinese jewelry company to supply 680 tons of amber in 3 years. Because of this, the demand for Ukrainian raw amber shrunk more than by half.

While in Kaliningrad mining sites the amber is excavated by machines, Ukrainian amber diggers still use shovels, or power pumps at best.

This competition made the Ukrainian amber price dwindle: a chunk of 100 g, the size of a fist, may cost USD 5-7 thousand. This is twice less than during the lucrative period of 2012-2014, but the amber diggers do not set their shovels and pumps aside.

The Polissya deposits amber is of better quality, but the Russians excavate more and sell cheaper.

To have an idea about the space of the areas destroyed by mining, we created and applied the machine algorhythm, which, after screening through several hundreds of thousands of satellite images, was the first one to determine key amber mining sites in the three Polissya oblasts.
